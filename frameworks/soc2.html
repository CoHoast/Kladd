Overview

What is SOC 2?

SOC 2 (Service Organization Control 2) is a security framework developed by the AICPA. It demonstrates that your organization has proper controls in place to protect customer data.

SOC 2 compliance is often required by enterprise customers, especially in B2B SaaS. It covers five Trust Service Criteria: Security, Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, and Privacy.

Type I evaluates your controls at a specific point in time. Type II evaluates them over a period (usually 3-12 months).

SOC 2 FAQs

Do I need SOC 2 Type I or Type II?
Type I is a point-in-time assessment — it's faster and good for startups who need to show initial compliance. Type II covers a period (3-12 months) and is more comprehensive. Most enterprise customers eventually require Type II, but many accept Type I initially.
Which Trust Service Criteria do I need?
Security is required for all SOC 2 audits. The others (Availability, Processing Integrity, Confidentiality, Privacy) depend on your business. Most B2B SaaS companies start with Security + Availability. Kladd helps you determine which ones you need based on your customers' requirements.
How much does SOC 2 certification cost?
The audit itself typically costs $15,000-$50,000 depending on your auditor and scope. With traditional approaches, you'd also spend $30,000-$100,000+ on consultants and tools. With Kladd starting at $999/month, you can significantly reduce preparation costs.
