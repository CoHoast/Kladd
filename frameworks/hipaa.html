Overview

What is HIPAA?

HIPAA (Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act) is a federal law that sets standards for protecting sensitive patient health information (PHI) from being disclosed without consent.

If you're building software that handles health data — telehealth, EHR integrations, health apps, or healthcare analytics — you need to be HIPAA compliant.

HIPAA has two main rules: the Privacy Rule (how PHI can be used/shared) and the Security Rule (technical safeguards to protect PHI).

HIPAA FAQs

Do I need HIPAA compliance?
If you're a Covered Entity (healthcare provider, health plan, or clearinghouse) or a Business Associate (you handle PHI on behalf of a Covered Entity), you need to be HIPAA compliant. Most digital health companies fall under the Business Associate category.
Is there a HIPAA "certification"?
There's no official HIPAA certification from the government. However, you can demonstrate compliance through third-party audits/assessments. Many customers accept a SOC 2 + HIPAA attestation, which combines both frameworks in one audit.
What happens if I'm not compliant?
HIPAA violations can result in significant penalties — from $100 to $50,000 per violation, with annual maximums up to $1.5M. Beyond fines, breaches damage customer trust and can end partnerships with healthcare organizations.
Does Kladd sign a BAA?
Yes! We're happy to sign a BAA with all customers using our HIPAA features. We take our role as a Business Associate seriously and maintain our own HIPAA compliance program.
