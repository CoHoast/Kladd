Overview

What is GDPR?

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is the EU's comprehensive data protection law. It applies to any organization that processes personal data of EU residents—regardless of where the company is based.

GDPR grants individuals rights over their data: access, rectification, erasure ("right to be forgotten"), portability, and more. Organizations must demonstrate compliance through documentation, impact assessments, and records of processing.

Non-compliance can result in fines up to €20 million or 4% of annual global revenue—whichever is higher.

Scope

Does GDPR apply to you?

If you answer yes to any of these, GDPR likely applies

Established in the EU

You have an office, subsidiary, or branch in any EU/EEA country. The GDPR applies to all personal data you process, even if processing happens outside the EU.

Targeting EU residents

You offer goods or services to people in the EU (even for free), or you monitor behavior of EU residents (e.g., tracking, profiling, analytics).

Processing for EU clients

You process personal data on behalf of EU companies as a data processor. You need a Data Processing Agreement and must follow GDPR requirements.

EU employees or customers

You have employees, contractors, or customers who are EU residents. Their personal data is protected by GDPR regardless of where you're based.

FAQ

Common GDPR questions

Do I need a DPO (Data Protection Officer)?

A DPO is mandatory if you're a public authority, process special category data at scale, or conduct large-scale systematic monitoring. Many companies appoint one voluntarily. Kladd can help document your DPO decision.

What's the difference between a controller and processor?

Controllers determine why and how data is processed. Processors handle data on behalf of controllers. Many SaaS companies are processors for their customers' data. Kladd helps you understand and document your role.

How do I handle international data transfers?

Post-Schrems II, you need valid transfer mechanisms: adequacy decisions, Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), or Binding Corporate Rules. Kladd tracks your transfers and documents compliance.

What triggers the 72-hour breach notification?

You must notify your supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of a breach that risks individuals' rights and freedoms. Kladd provides breach assessment workflows and notification templates.

Can GDPR compliance help with other regulations?

Yes! GDPR overlaps with CCPA/CPRA (California), LGPD (Brazil), and other privacy laws. Kladd maps controls across frameworks so you build once and comply everywhere.

What records do I need to maintain?

Article 30 requires Records of Processing Activities (RoPA). You also need consent records, DPIA documentation, breach logs, and evidence of your accountability measures. Kladd generates all of these automatically.
