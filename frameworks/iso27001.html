Overview

What is ISO 27001?

ISO 27001 is the international standard for information security management systems (ISMS). Published by the International Organization for Standardization, it's recognized worldwide as the gold standard for security.

Unlike SOC 2, ISO 27001 requires formal certification by an accredited body. The certification is valid for 3 years with annual surveillance audits to maintain compliance.

ISO 27001 is often required for international business, especially in Europe, government contracts, and enterprise sales. It demonstrates a mature, systematic approach to security.

Common ISO 27001 questions

How much does ISO 27001 certification cost?

Total cost depends on company size. Certification audit fees typically range from $15,000-$50,000. With Kladd, you save 60-80% on preparation costs by automating documentation and evidence collection.

What's the difference between ISO 27001 and ISO 27002?

ISO 27001 is the certifiable standard that specifies requirements. ISO 27002 is guidance on implementing the controls. Kladd incorporates both—the 27001 requirements and 27002 best practices.

Do I need a consultant for ISO 27001?

Traditionally yes, costing $50K-$150K. Kladd replaces most consultant work with automation. Our AI generates your ISMS documentation and guides implementation step-by-step.

What are surveillance audits?

After initial certification, you'll have annual surveillance audits (years 1 and 2) and a full recertification audit in year 3. Kladd keeps you continuously audit-ready with automated monitoring.

Can I get ISO 27001 and SOC 2 together?

Absolutely. About 70% of controls overlap. Kladd maps evidence to both frameworks simultaneously, so you only do the work once. Our Scale plan includes both frameworks.

Which certification body should I use?

Choose an accredited certification body (CB) from UKAS, ANAB, or similar. We can recommend CPs we've worked with. The CB must be independent—Kladd is not a certification body.
